Logics and Directions

How the names were chosen.

The principles behind the stack. Each holds the system together and carries the upgrade argument on its own.

Plain modifiers, ORTH-owned destination.
The ladder uses universal SaaS grammar. Pro is Pro. The work happens at the top, where Agronomist names not a feature but a relationship. The grower does not buy a higher tier. The grower works with their own agronomist.
The destination earns its weight.
Agronomist sits at the top of the individual stack as the qualitative jump from the modifier-laddered tiers below it. Agronomist Pro extends it. Teams Agronomist and Teams Agronomist Pro mirror the same logic across a crew. The word arrives at the point where the work becomes serious, not as a label applied uniformly to every tier.
Vertical, not LLM-shaped.
The product is agronomic, and the names should read agronomic. Foundation AI, Advanced AI, and Expert AI are placeholders that describe a wrapper around a model. Surface, Command, and Adaptive describe the work. The grower invokes the layer that matches the weight of the decision in front of them, not a model class.
Tiers name where you are. Layers name what you call for.
Surface, Command, and Adaptive cut across tiers. Surface is the everyday read. Command is full reasoning when the decision matters. Adaptive is research-grade analysis tuned to the question. Tier is identity. Layer is attention. The two do not need to align.
Teams mirrors the individual ladder.
Teams begins where the crew starts working the field together, with a parallel ascent: Teams, Teams Agronomist, Teams Agronomist Pro. Same destination, applied across a roster. The shared version of ORTH is not a separate hierarchy. It is the same hierarchy, multiplied by seats.
Pronounceable, defensible, native.
Agronomist is the English word for the profession the tier is named after. No foreign-language read. No ambiguity in pronunciation. No defense required in a meeting. The destination is named what the destination is.
Every call has a consequence.
The name is the first call ORTH makes on the grower's behalf.
