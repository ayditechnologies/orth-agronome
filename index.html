Logics and Directions

How the names were chosen.

The principles behind the stack. Each holds the system together and carries the upgrade argument on its own.

Stages, not tiers.
ORTH does not sell products ranked by price. It names the stages of a grower's path. Ground is where you stand. Field Plus is where you work. Agronome is who you become. Agronome Pro is the mastery of the practice. The ladder exists, but the words describe identity, not position.
The word does the work.
A grower on Ground hears Field Plus as a natural next step. A grower on Field Plus hears Agronome as what they are already becoming. The name carries the upgrade argument on its own, without a ladder of modifiers like Plus, Plus Max, Expert. Each tier stands as its own sentence, not a position on a scale.
Agronome is the family identity.
The word carries across four products: Agronome, Agronome Pro, Teams Agronome, Agronome Atlas. The identity is shared. The modifiers each name a different vector — Pro names depth of practice, Teams names scale across a crew, Atlas names institutional reach. One word does the work of four distinctions.
Tiers name where you are. Layers name what you call for.
Surface, Command, and Adaptive cut across tiers. None of the three says AI. Each describes what the grower is invoking in the moment: the light read for everyday questions, full reasoning for decisions that matter, research-grade analysis when the question demands it. Tier is identity. Layer is attention. The two do not need to align.
Teams begin where the crew works together.
Teams tiers do not start at Ground. A single grower starts on Ground. A crew starts on Teams Field. The shared version of ORTH begins where the field becomes collective, not where one person stands. This is why there is no Teams Ground, and why Teams Horizon carries its own season-level frame rather than mirroring the individual ladder word for word.
Atlas carries the weight of the word.
Agronome Atlas is not self-serve. It is reserved for universities, research institutes, agribusiness R&D, and government programmes. The word Atlas earns its meaning: the complete map, the held territory, the view an institution needs when funding a research programme or publishing peer-reviewed agronomic findings. Naming Atlas as a separate crown, rather than a higher Pro, protects the integrity of Agronome Pro below it.
No AI in the names, anywhere.
Foundation AI, Advanced AI, Expert AI were placeholders that described the wrapper. ORTH is not a large language model and should not speak like one. Surface, Command, and Adaptive describe the work, not the mechanism. The grower does not buy a model. The grower buys an agronomist's practice, scaled.
Every call has a consequence.
The name is the first call ORTH makes on the grower's behalf.
